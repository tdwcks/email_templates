Shareholder Update: Spring 2017

Dear Shareholder,

Welcome to the first quarterly update of 2017. Spring has arrived and it's a good moment to look back on a busy winter. We’ve got lots of good news to report and some challenges to reflect on. With global launch on the horizon, this year is set to be very exciting. We hope you’re keen to get stuck in where you can and share your experience, networks and time to make this a huge year for the business.

We’ll start with the challenges. December saw us withdraw our raise from Crowdcube. As we said at the time, we accept that was due to the lack of a lead investor. With over £200k pledged through our pre-registration page we had crowd support, but we didn’t have an institution to drive confidence in the round. It was a difficult month or two for us. Our belief in the business never faltered, but we had to acknowledge we showed our inexperience raising money.

After the difficulties of December, the new year brought much happier news. We sat down with many of you, talked about our plans, took your feedback onboard and put a clearer proposition forward. We would raise a round to build for Android and develop our partnership offering - key to growth. We closed that round by the end of January and we are now well on our way with both. Thanks for the passion and support that you all showed.

Alongside investment we’ve also received a small amount of non-equity funding through Sport London’s Sportivate program, in partnership with London Athletics which we’ll soon begin investing/deploying to acquire young, inactive users. User growth has been great since the turn of the year - we’ve had nearly 6,000 new users join us organically.

The Headlines

Great

  • Closed funding round in January.

  • Have started building for Android - expecting beta launch in May.

  • Awarded London Athletics funding to work with young people.

  • Ran a user survey - 42% of the people using RAE say it has made them more active and of them, 48% were previously at risk. Great for social impact funding.

  • We now know that our user group is generally 10 years younger than the “first generation” self quantification fitness tracker apps (Runkeeper et al).

Good

  • We’ve appointed Professional Sports Group as partnerships lead, reflecting its importance for us as a business. They represent the Boat Race and Tom Daley among other clients.

  • Biggest quarter for acquisition since launch as we pass 24,000 installs + we’ve had just over 10,000 unique users this year so far.

  • Press coverage - Coach, EuroGamer, Evening Standard, Telegraph.

  • Finalist in the Sports Technology Awards under best participation technology.

  • We’ve been selected on to the Mayor's International Business Programme, designed for companies set to scale globally this year.

Upcoming

  • Agreeing terms with strategic partners for our territory by territory global roll-out.

  • Building a “where should we launch next” page to drive pre-launch engagement and assess the market appetite.

  • We’re building out a long term partnership with Men’s and Women’s Running. We’re filming the introductory video this week.

  • We’ll be putting Android into Beta in the next 8 weeks.

Investor thanks

  • James Lemon for giving up time to help with a number of advisory sessions.

  • Henry Yates for being a great sounding board as we reshaped the proposition.

  • Anna Hyde for helping us with paperwork and emergency legal support.

  • Olivia and Leo Cuturi for ongoing help shaping how we present the business.

How can you help

  • Partners - Partnerships are integral to our growth strategy. If you have connections at Sports Brands, Health Insurers, Telecoms or Corporates with HR departments, then we would love meet for a coffee and discuss how we can help meet their business objectives.

  • C-Suite and Board - In the next 6 months as we prepare for larger raises, we would love to add to our management and governance capacity. If you know someone who has exited from a start-up, has experience investing in them or worked in sports and leisure and think they would make a valuable addition to the team then please do put us in touch.

  • Also, get in touch if you have any general feedback or thoughts, an objective view of the business is always appreciated. If there is something we’re not doing that you think we should, or something we’re doing well and should do more of we’d love to know about it.

All the best,

Ben, Sam and the Run An Empire team.
